Telling Time in Thai

:
Notes:

    So what the heck is going on with time in Thai?

    Roughly speaking, the day is divided into 5 chunks, each of which has its own word to identify which chunk you're in:

    • 1am to 5am (ตี followed by the numbers 1-5)
    • 6am to 11am (the number followed by โมงเช้า)
    • 1pm to 4pm (บ่าย followed by the number)
    • 4pm to 6pm (the numbers 4-6 followed by โมงเย็น)
    • 7pm to 11pm (the numbers 1-5 followed by ทุ่ม)

    There are a few things to point out here. First, yes, 4pm shows up twice. It can follow either pattern, although I've found 4โมงเย็น a little more common than บ่าย4โมง.

    Second, from 7am to 11am, either the numbers 7-11 can be used, or the numbers 1-5. I've found 1-5 to be a little more common, but it's good to recognize both patterns.

    Third, for 1pm, there is no number, just บ่ายโมง. For 2pm to 4pm, the numbers 2-4 follow บ่าย.

    Finally, noon and midnight have their own words: เที่ยง for noon and เที่ยงคืน for midnight.
